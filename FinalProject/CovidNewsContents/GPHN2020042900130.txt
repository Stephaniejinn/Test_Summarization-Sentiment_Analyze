Title : Coronavirus latest: Here's what you need to know this Wednesday morning, April 29
Ariticle : 
Here are the key coronavirusupdates from thelast 24-hours.
A NORTH-EAST NHS Trust recorded its 100thcoronavirus patient death as a further 32 die in hospitals across the region. Latest figures published on Tuesday afternoon confirmed a further 552 people across England have died after testing positive for Covid-19. The Gateshead HealthcareNHSFoundation Trust saw an increase of nine patient deaths in 24 hours bringing its death toll to 100. British Airways is set to make up to 12,000 workers redundant, parent company IAG has announced. The airline, which employs 42,000 people, has suffered from the global collapse in passenger numbers caused by the coronavirus pandemic. IAG said BA will consult on a "restructuring and redundancy programme" as it is expected to take "several years" until demand for air travel returns to 2019 levels. Professor Angela McLean, the deputy chief scientific adviser, said the latest data showed coronavirus hospitaladmissions and deaths in hospitals were falling. Speaking to a press conference at Downing Street, she said the number of people in hospital with Covid-19 had fallen by 14 percentin the past week. A drug to treat the virusis entering an early clinical trial phase. Matt Hancock said a UK therapeutics taskforce working to see if current drugs can be effectively deployed against the disease have identified a number of "promising candidates". The Government is "on track" to hit the 100,000 daily testing target by the end of the month. Care homes across the North-East andNorth Yorkshirehave recorded almost 400 coronavirus-related deaths in just two weeks. Between April 10 and April 24, carehomesin the region hadinformed the Care Quality Commission (CQC)of 377 deaths which involved Covid-19. The Office forNationalStatistics (ONS), which records the number of registered deaths, released the latest figures on Tuesday morning. It is the first time the CQC death notifications for suspected or confirmed Covid-19 in care homes have been published.
