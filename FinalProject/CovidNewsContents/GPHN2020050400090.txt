Title : Perth headlines round-up May 04, 0530
Ariticle : 
Here's what is making news across Western Australia...
* Residents of southwestern Western Australia have been warned of a "significant" cold weather front, including high winds, that will cross the state.
* Only 19 cases of coronavirus remain active in Western Australia but the death toll has climbed to nine.
* The fatal shooting of a FIFO worker during a stabbing frenzy in a regional West Australian shopping centre was a textbook take-down by police, the union says.
The latest headlines in sport...
* English Premier League clubs are divided over the health and safety of restarting the season amid growing concerns over finances.
* Captain Roger Tuivasa-Sheck says the Warriors will keep their "eyes on the prize" when the NRL resumes despite admitting time away from home will be tough.
* Some Scottish soccer clubs are preparing in case the 2020-21 soccer is cancelled, Scottish Football Association vice-president Mike Mulraney says.
Here are this morning's latest national and world headlines...
* A Sydney public school has closed after a student tested positive to coronavirus, days after term 2 started.
* Disability support workers have had poor access to personal protective equipment and other health precautions during the pandemic, a new survey reveals.
* Brazil has now registered 101,147 confirmed cases of the virus and 7025 deaths, the country's health ministry has confirmed.
* Chinese leaders "intentionally concealed the severity" of the coronavirus pandemic from the world, according to a US Department of Homeland Security report.
