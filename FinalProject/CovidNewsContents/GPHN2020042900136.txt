Title : Record 1,840 cases & 69 deaths on Tuesday; Covid toll crosses 1,000
Ariticle : 
The Covid-19 pandemic crossed two grim milestones in India, with the total number of cases topping 30,000 - ending the day at 31,317 - and the death toll going past 1,000. Both the number of new cases, at 1,840, and fatalities (69) on Tuesday were the highest reported in a single day so far.
Maharashtra recorded its highest daily death toll of 31, followed by Gujarat (19), Madhya Pradesh (10) and UP (3). Mumbai and Ahmedabad saw their highest number of deaths in a day, 25 and 19, respectively.
Tamil Nadu saw its highest number of new cases(121), while Gujarat reported 226 and Delhi 206.
India added 1,840 new coronavirus infections, going past the previous single-day peak of 1,755 recorded on April 23.
Rajasthan and Jammu & Kashmir recorded two new Covid-19 deaths each while Karnataka and Tamil Nadu recorded one fatality each. Delhi did not record any death for the second consecutive day. However, with 206 more persons testing positive for the disease, Delhi's Covid-19 count has now reached 3,314, the third highest in the country after Maharashtra (9,318) and Gujarat (3,774).
Madhya Pradesh recorded 10 deaths from Covid-19 infection - one of the highest single-day tolls in the state since the outbreak. Indore again reported a rush of cases, with 94 added to the city's tally, taking it past 1,300. Indore and Ujjain also recorded three deaths each. The state's death toll has reached 120, third highest in the country.
In Uttar Pradesh, three more Covid-19 deaths were reported, two from Agra and one from Kanpur. The state also recorded 66 new cases, taking its tally to 2029. Two persons died due to Covid-19 in Rajasthan while 102 were tested positive for the viral infection in the state.
An 11-month-old Infant from Kurnool tested positive as Andhra Pradesh on Tuesday reported 82 new Covid-19 positive cases, the highest single-day spike so far, taking the total count to 1,259.
Telangana continued to report single-digit numbers with only six new Covid-19 cases. The overall coronovirus case load I the state now stands at 1009. Jharkhand also got some respite on Tuesday as the state recorded just two new cases.
