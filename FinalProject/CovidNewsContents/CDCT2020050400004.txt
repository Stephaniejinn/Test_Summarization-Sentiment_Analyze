Title : 1 new confirmed diagnosis, moved overseas-Department of Health and Welfare Disease Control Department

Release Date: 2020-05-04
The Central Epidemic Epidemic Command Center announced today (4) that there is a new case of COVID-19 (Wuhan pneumonia) moved overseas in China (case 437), which is a woman in her 30s. She will go to school in the United States on January 30 and enter on April 9. It was asymptomatic at the time, and was diagnosed by passengers on the same flight during the home quarantine (case 383), and was reclassified as home isolation on April 11.

The command center stated that the case of 437 home seizures only showed symptoms of runny nose on April 15th, which was considered to be allergic and not reported. After the expiration date on April 24th, the case took the initiative to contact the health unit on April 27th to indicate that they had runny nose 1. Symptoms of nasal congestion are arranged by the health unit for medical examination. Due to the weak positive result of the test, they were admitted to the negative pressure isolation ward on April 30. To be cautious, they were collected on April 30, May 1 and 2 respectively. The test was sent for examination and the diagnosis was confirmed today; the case is currently asymptomatic and in hospital isolation.
