Title : Asia-Pacific Coronavirus News: Japan extends state of emergency until May 31
Ariticle : 
People wearing masks walk on the street in Nagoya, Japan, April 17, 2020. (Kyodo News/Handout via Xinhua)
-- Japan extends state of emergency until May 31 for COVID-19 fight;
-- Bangladesh reports 688 new cases, total at 10,143;
-- Thailand announces 18 new cases, tally at 2,987;
-- Infections in Indonesia increased by 395 to 11,587.
HONG KONG, May 4 (Xinhua) -- The following are the latest developments of the COVID-19 pandemic in Asia-Pacific countries.
TOKYO -- The Japanese government on Monday officially announced its decision to extend the nationwide state of emergency for COVID-19 by nearly a month until May 31.
Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe, scheduled to hold a press conference at 6:00 p.m. local time, made the formal decision to extend the state of emergency beyond May 6, the last day of Japan's Golden Week holiday.
People queue up to have a test for COVID-19 at a hospital in Dhaka, Bangladesh, on May 3, 2020. (Str/Xinhua)
DHAKA -- Bangladesh on Monday reported a record daily jump of 688 new COVID-19 cases, taking its total to 10,143.
Five more deaths were reported in the last 24 hours, taking the death toll to 182.
People line up to pick up food at a free food distribution point in Bangkok, Thailand, April 22, 2020. (Photo by Rachen Sageamsak/Xinhua)
BANGKOK -- Thailand's daily new COVID-19 infections rose to 18 on Monday, compared with three cases on Sunday, and all of them were illegal immigrants held at a detention center in Thailand's Southern Province of Songkhla.
No deaths were reported, and the total number of infections stood at 2,987, while 2,740 people have recovered, 193 remained in hospitals. The total number of deaths stood at 54.
Vendors wearing face masks sell snacks during Ramadan in Jakarta, Indonesia, April 28, 2020. (Photo by Zulkarnain/Xinhua)
JAKARTA -- Indonesia has reported 395 new COVID-19 cases, bringing the total to 11,587, the government's health official said at a press conference on Monday afternoon.
A total of 864 deaths have been recorded in the country, an increase by 19 over the previous day.
A medical staff member prepares to enter a building under the enhanced movement control order (EMCO) in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, April 15, 2020. 
(Photo by Chong Voon Chung/Xinhua)
KUALA LUMPUR -- Malaysia reported 55 new COVID-19 cases, among which seven were imported cases with the remainder being local transmissions, pushing the total cases in the country to 6,353.
A worker delivers food during Labor Day in Makati, the Philippines, on May 1, 2020. (Xinhua/Rouelle Umali)
MANILA -- In the Philippines, the number of confirmed COVID-19 cases rose by 262, to a total of 9,485, the country's health authorities said.
A total of 101 more patients recovered from the virus, raising the country's total number of recoveries to 1,315, and 16 new deaths were recorded in the last 24 hours, bringing the death toll to 623.
A vendor sells fruits during the COVID-19 pandemic in Vientiane, Laos, April 3, 2020. (Photo by Kaikeo Saiyasane/Xinhua)
VIENTIANE -- Laos confirmed no new COVID-19 cases for 22 consecutive days, with the total number of confirmed cases remaining at 19.
As of Monday, Laos has tested 2,223 suspected cases with 19 cases tested positive, and nine patients have recovered.
People rest by the river in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, March 26, 2020. (Photo by Ou Zhengjin/Xinhua)
PHNOM PENH -- Blue Sky Rescue Team, one of non-governmental organizations (NGO) in China, has sent a team of 10 volunteers and donated medical supplies to Cambodia to help fight the COVID-19 pandemic.
A volunteer teaches English online during curfew in Kandahar province, southern Afghanistan, April 18, 2020. (Xinhua/Sanaullah Seiam)
KABUL -- Afghanistan's health ministry on Monday reported 190 new COVID-19 cases within the past 24 hours, taking the total cases to 2,894.
Five COVID patients died since early Sunday, bringing the death toll to 90.
An Indian woman lights earthen lamps on her balcony to mark the country's fight against the COVID-19 pandemic in New Delhi, India, April 5, 2020. (Xinhua/Javed Dar)
NEW DELHI -- India on Monday morning reported 67 more COVID-19 deaths and 2,270 new positive cases since Sunday evening in the country, taking the number of deaths to 1,373 and total cases to 42,533.
According to ministry officials, 11,707 people have been discharged from hospitals after showing improvement.
Photo taken on March 30, 2020 shows toys displayed at a window of a house in Wellington, New Zealand. 
(Xinhua/Guo Lei)
WELLINGTON -- New Zealand reported no new COVID-19 case over the past 24 hours, the first time in 49 days, with the total number of infections staying at 1,487, the Ministry of Health said on Monday.
A policeman is on duty at a temporary checkpoint on Vatuwaqa Bridge in Suva, Fiji, April 11, 2020. (Xinhua/Zhang Yongxing)
SUVA -- Fiji's Health Ministry said on Monday that Fijians cannot afford to become complacent given that many countries are reporting a resurgence of coronavirus (COVID-19) cases.
Fiji's Health Minister Ifereimi Waqainabete said lifting of COVID-19 restrictions will not mean that life will totally return to what it was before the pandemic, and for the time-being limitations will remain in some situations.
People enjoying their weekend life near Hongik University in Seoul, South Korea, May 3, 2020. (Xinhua/Wang Jingqiang)
SEOUL -- South Korea reported eight more cases of the COVID-19 compared to 24 hours ago as of 0:00 a.m. Monday local time, raising the total number of infections to 10,801.
The daily caseload hovered below 20 for 17 straight days. All the new cases were imported from overseas, lifting the combined number to 1,099.
Surfers walk at Bondi Beach in Sydney, Australia, on April 28, 2020. (Xinhua/Bai Xuefei)
SYDNEY -- A jump in the number of COVID-19 cases in Australia has been largely linked to a meat processing plant in the State of Victoria, authorities revealed on Monday.
Victorian Premier Daniel Andrews told reporters that of the 22 new cases reported on Monday, 19 were workers at a meat processing plant, or slaughterhouse, taking the state's total to 1,406.
