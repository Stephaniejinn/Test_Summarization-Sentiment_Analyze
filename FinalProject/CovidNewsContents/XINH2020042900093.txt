Title : Asia-Pacific Coronavirus News: Cases in Japan rise by 202 to 14,097, Bangladesh's cases exceed 7,000 with daily record rise
Ariticle : 
People wearing masks walk on the street in Nagoya, Japan, April 17, 2020. (Kyodo News/Handout via Xinhua)
-- Cases in Japan rise by 202 to 14,097.
-- Bangladesh's cases exceed 7,000 with daily record rise.
-- Cases in Philippines surpass 8,000, with 254 new cases.
-- Malaysia reports spike in imported COVID-19 cases.
HONG KONG, April 29 (Xinhua) -- The following are the latest developments of the COVID-19 pandemic in Asia-Pacific countries.
TOKYO -- The confirmed COVID-19 cases in Japan increased by 202 to reach 14,097, according to the latest figures from the health ministry and local authorities on Wednesday.
A soldier stands guard next to a community under the enhanced movement control order in Selayang Baru, Selangor, Malaysia, on April 26, 2020. (Photo by Chong Voon Chung/Xinhua)
KUALA LUMPUR -- Malaysia reported 94 new COVID-19 cases, most of them being imported cases of locals returning from overseas, the Health Ministry said on Wednesday.
Health Ministry Director-General Noor Hisham Abdullah said at a press briefing that 72 of the 94 new cases were Malaysians returning from Indonesia
Photo taken on April 23, 2020 shows a staff spraying disinfectant in Bangladesh's National Zoo in Dhaka, Bangladesh. (Str/Xinhua)
DHAKA -- Bangladesh has registered a daily record of 641 COVID-19 cases over the last 24 hours, with the total number exceeding 7,000 as of Wednesday, official data showed.
A policeman from the Philippine National Police (PNP) checks the body temperature of a motorist at a checkpoint in Manila, the Philippines, April 24, 2020. (Xinhua/Rouelle Umali)
MANILA -- The number of coronavirus cases in the Philippines climbed to 8,212 on Wednesday, with 254 new cases reported amid strict lockdown restrictions imposed since mid-March.
People ride at Sisters Bay in Sydney, Australia on April 23, 2020. (Xinhua/Bai Xuefei)
SYDNEY -- An experimental COVID-19 vaccine has shown it can stimulate a strong enough immune response to kill the virus in preclinical trials, Australian researchers said on Wednesday.
Vendors wearing face masks sell snacks during Ramadan in Jakarta, Indonesia, April 28, 2020. (Photo by Zulkarnain/Xinhua)
JAKARTA -- The Indonesian government on Wednesday announced 260 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, bringing the total number of infections in the archipelagic country to 9,771.
Indonesian President Joko Widodo said the government has decided to exempt the final income tax for small and medium enterprises (SMEs) and provide financial assistance to capitals.
A farmer wearing a face mask works in the field in Agartala, capital city of India's northeastern state of Tripura, during a government-imposed nationwide lockdown as a preventive measure against the COVID-19, April 27, 2020. (Str/Xinhua)
NEW DELHI -- India's federal health ministry Wednesday morning said 70 new deaths due to COVID-19 and an additional 1,358 positive cases were reported since Tuesday evening across the country, taking the number of deaths to 1,007 and total cases to 31,332.
People exercise on a beach in Wellington, New Zealand, April 28, 2020. (Xinhua/Guo Lei)
WELLINGTON -- New Zealand reported two new confirmed COVID-19 cases and one probable case over the past 24 hours, with the total number of infections being 1,474 in the country, the Ministry of Health said on Wednesday.
There is no more death reported as the COVID-19 death toll remained at 19 in the country, Director-General of Health Ashley Bloomfield told a press conference.
A man walks past a COVID-19 fight poster in the capital city of Hanoi, Vietnam, April 1, 2020. (VNA/Handout via Xinhua)
HANOI -- Vietnam reported on Wednesday that a patient in the country has tested positive again for COVID-19 after a full recovery, Vietnam News Agency reported.
The slogan "We're All Heroes !" honoring all Seoul citizens during the fight against COVID-19 pandemic, written in both Korean and English is seen on a lawn in Seoul, South Korea, April 23, 2020. (Seoul Metropolitan Government/Handout via Xinhua)
SEOUL -- South Korea reported nine more cases of the COVID-19 compared to 24 hours ago as of 0:00 a.m. Wednesday local time, raising the total number of infections to 10,761.
The daily caseload hovered around 10 for the 11th straight day.
Two more deaths were confirmed, raising the death toll to 246. The total fatality rate came in at 2.29 percent.
A policeman wearing a mask stands guard outside the Insein Prison in Yangon, Myanmar, April 17, 2020. (Xinhua/U Aung)
YANGON -- The total number of COVID-19 infections in Myanmar has risen to 149, with three more confirmed cases reported late Tuesday, according to a release from the Ministry of Health and Sports.
Myanmar has reported five deaths of COVID-19 as of Tuesday since the infectious disease was first detected in the country on March 23.
Photo taken on April 2, 2020 shows social distancing markers in a cafe in Singapore's Marina Bay area. (Photo by Then Chih Wey/Xinhua)
SINGAPORE -- Singapore's Ministry of Health (MOH) reported 528 new COVID-19 cases on Tuesday, bringing the total confirmed cases in the country to 14,951.
Of the newly confirmed cases, 10 were detected in the community, seven were work permit holders residing outside dormitories, and 511 were work permit holders residing in dormitories.
