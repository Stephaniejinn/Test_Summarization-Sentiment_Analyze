Ottawa sees biggest single-day COVID-19 spike: 67 cases, 12 dead
There have been 59 coronavirus-related deaths in Ottawa institutions -- 71 across the city, to date.
By: Mike Vlasveld
Stock photo
Ottawa Public Health (OPH) is confirming 67 new lab-confirmed cases of COVID-19 in the city and another 12 deaths caused by the virus.
That brings the city's total case number to 1,221 (with 529 resolved), and 71 dead.
OPH is reporting 59 coronavirus-related deaths in Ottawa long-term care homes, retirement residences and hospitals, Tuesday.
The health agency's updated figures show Montfort Long-Term Care Centre with a death toll of 16, while 15 have died at Madonna Care Community.
Tuesday's info from OPH still only shows eight deaths at Carlingview Manor after the home confirmed last weekend that they had dealt with 11 COVID-19 deaths.
Overall, OPH says 517 cases of COVID-19 have been found in residents or staff members at local institutions.
Since April 14, the number of patients in Ottawa intensive care units with the virus has consistently dropped from more than 17, now down to just 6. There are 35 people in Ottawa hospitals with COVID-19, according to OPH.
