Title : Another 5 Covid-19 patients were discharged from the hospital, of which 2 were re-positive
Ariticle : 

The patients are expected to be discharged tomorrow morning (5/5). These patients include: BN074, BN137, BN233, BN245, BN267.


Notably, among the patients expected to be discharged tomorrow there are two cases of re-positive cases after being announced from the previous cure, namely:

BN074 is a male from Phu Tho province. The patient was discharged from the hospital on April 20 and was released home isolated. During isolation, patients have no fever, no cough, no shortness of breath. On April 25, after 14 days of home isolation, the patient who was sampled for testing, suspected to be positive for SARS-CoV-2 virus should be referred to the Central Hospital for Tropical Diseases. After 4 negative results for SARS-CoV-2 virus (since re-positive), BN074 is expected to be discharged on 5/5.

BN137 is a male from Nghe An province, who was declared cured on 7 April and continues to be isolated from health monitoring for another 14 days as required. On April 22, after 6 negative tests, he was sampled for the 7th time and was discharged home in Nghe An. However, after the test results of the Central Institute of Hygiene and Epidemiology showed that patients positive for SARS-CoV-2 should be taken back to the Central Tropical Hospital. After 4 negative results for SARS-CoV-2 (since re-positive), BN137 is expected to be discharged on 5/5.



BN137 (right cover, previous row) on the day of previous disease recovery.



With 5 more cases expected to be discharged on 5/5, Vietnam has 224 Covid-19 patients who have recovered, 47 cases continue to treat, of which 12 are re-positive patients with SARS- CoV-2.


On the morning of 4/5, Vietnam has spent 18 days without detecting new cases of infection due to infection in the community, currently the total number of Covid-19 cases recorded in Vietnam is 271. The latest case to be published as specialized British oil and gas enterprises were also quarantined upon entry. The remaining patients have relatively stable health status. However, the case of the British pilot in Ho Chi Minh City is very worrying, when health progresses worse, pneumothorax.
