Title : India COVID-19 death toll rises to 1,075 as total cases reach 33,610
Ariticle : 
A COVID-19 sample collection kiosk is seen on a road during a government-imposed nationwide lockdown as a preventive measure against COVID-19 in Agartala, the capital city of India's northeastern state of Tripura, April 28, 2020. (Str/Xinhua)
One new death and 560 positive cases were reported in India since Thursday morning, taking the number of deaths to 1,075 and total cases to 33,610.
NEW DELHI, May 1 (Xinhua) -- India's federal health ministry Thursday evening said one new death due to COVID-19, besides an additional 560 positive cases were reported since Thursday morning across the country, taking the number of deaths to 1,075 and total cases to 33,610.
"As on 5:00 p.m. (local time), today 1,075 deaths related to novel coronavirus have been recorded in the country," reads information released by the ministry.
On Thursday morning the number of COVID-19 cases in the country was 33,050 and the death toll was 1,074.
According to ministry officials, so far 8,373 people have been discharged from hospitals after showing improvement.
"The number of active cases in the country right now is 24,162," reads the information.
Thursday marks the 37th straight day of ongoing lockdown across the country announced by the government to contain the spread of the pandemic.
The lockdown which was announced on March 25 has been extended until May 3.
The ministry said the coronavirus recovery rate - the share of people who have been discharged from hospital after treatment - has risen to 25.13 percent, a significant improvement over 13 percent reported 14 days ago. The doubling rate of COVID-19 cases has also improved to 11 days from 3.4 days before the nationwide lockdown.
"Analysis of doubling rate across the country has revealed that the national average is 11 days presently vis-a-vis 3.4 days before lockdown," the health ministry said.
Meanwhile, Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi held a meeting to discuss strategies to attract more foreign investments into the country as well as to promote local investments in order to give a boost to the economy against the backdrop of the COVID-19 pandemic.
"Various strategies to bring investments into India in a fast-track mode and to promote Indian domestic sectors were discussed. Detailed discussions were held on guiding states to evolve their strategies and be more proactive in attracting investments," a statement issued by Prime Minister's Office said.
