Title : Asia-Pacific Coronavirus News: COVID-19 cases near 9,500 in Bangladesh with biggest daily jump of 665 new patients

HONG KONG, May 3 (Xinhua) -- The following are the latest developments of the COVID-19 pandemic in Asia-Pacific countries.
DHAKA -- Bangladesh reported 665 new COVID-19 cases Sunday, the biggest daily jump in new cases since March 8 when confirmed cases were first reported in the country.
A total of 665 new COVID-19 positive cases and two deaths were reported in the last 24 hours across Bangladesh, taking the total number of positive cases to 9,455 and death toll to 177.
A man recites verses from the holy Quran at a mosque in Dhaka, Bangladesh on April 30, 2020. (Str/Xinhua)
KUALA LUMPUR -- Malaysia reported 122 new COVID-19 cases, bringing the total to 6,298, of which 70 are local transmissions and 52 are imported cases.
Soldiers and police are on duty in front of a building under the enhanced movement control order (EMCO) in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, April 15, 2020. 
(Xinhua/Zhu Wei)
JAKARTA -- The Indonesian government on Sunday announced 349 new confirmed cases of COVID-19, bringing the total number of infections in the country to 11,192.
There were also 14 new deaths, taking the total number of fatalities to 845, and as many as 211 more people have been discharged from hospital after their recoveries, taking the total number of recovered patients to 1,876.
Vendors wearing face masks sell snacks during Ramadan in Jakarta, Indonesia, April 28, 2020. (Photo by Zulkarnain/Xinhua)
MANILA -- The total number of COVID-19 cases in the Philippines has climbed to 9,223, with 295 new infections reported on Sunday.
A total of 1,214 patients have recovered after recording 90 new recoveries, and four more patients died, taking the death toll to 607.
Workers from Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) of the Philippines prepare containers that will be converted into temporary medical facilities in Manila, the Philippines, on April 29, 2020. (Xinhua/Rouelle Umali)
BANGKOK-- Thailand on Sunday reported three new COVID-19 cases and no death.
So far, Thailand has reported a total of 2,969 coronavirus cases and 54 deaths since the outbreak began in January.
People line up to pick up food at a free food distribution point in Bangkok, Thailand, April 22, 2020. (Photo by Rachen Sageamsak/Xinhua)
KABUL -- Afghanistan's Public Health Ministry has registered 235 new COVID-19 positive cases over the past 24 hours totaling the number of patients infected with the virus to 2,704.
A total of 12 patients have died over the past 24 hours, bringing the number of deaths to 85 since the outbreak of the disease in February.
Afghan women make facemasks at a tailoring shop in Sari Pul province, Afghanistan, April 13, 2020. (Photo by Mohammad Jan Aria/Xinhua)
NEW DELHI -- India recorded 78 new deaths due to COVID-19, and 2,204 positive cases in the country, taking the number of deaths to 1,301 and total cases to 39,980.
People line up and get body temperature checked before entering a market during a government-imposed nationwide lockdown as a preventive measure against COVID-19 in Agartala, the capital city of India's northeastern state of Tripura, April 28, 2020. (Str/Xinhua)
WELLINGTON -- New Zealand reported two new confirmed cases of COVID-19, adding number of COVID-19 cases in the country to 1,487, the Ministry of Health said on Sunday.
Both of Sunday's cases can be linked to a cluster outbreak in an aged care facility in Auckland. Both cases are household contacts of an employee at the rest home.
The combined total of confirmed and probable cases in New Zealand is 1,487, including 1,136 confirmed cases. Currently, there are 1,266 people being recovered from COVID-19, while eight people are in hospital, reported the Ministry of Health in a statement.
Photo taken on April 18, 2020 shows a waiting area converted from a parking lot for customers at a supermarket in Wellington, New Zealand. 
(Xinhua/Guo Lei)
SEOUL -- South Korea reported 13 more cases of the COVID-19 compared to 24 hours ago as of 0:00 a.m. Sunday local time, raising the total number of infections to 10,793.
The daily caseload hovered below 20 for 16 straight days. Of the new cases, 10 were imported from overseas, lifting the combined number to 1,091.
No more death was confirmed, leaving the death toll at 250. The total fatality rate stood at 2.32 percent.
Voters stand in line with a social distance as they enter a polling station in Seoul, South Korea, April 15, 2020. 
(Xinhua/Lee Sang-ho)
SINGAPORE -- Singapore's Ministry of Health (MOH) reported 447 new COVID-19 cases on Saturday, bringing the total confirmed cases in the country to 17,548.
Of the newly confirmed cases, six were in the community, two were work permit holders residing outside dormitories, and 441 were work permit holders residing in dormitories. There were no imported cases.
Of the new cases, 93 percent are linked to known clusters, while the rest are pending contact tracing.
Workers tape up chairs and tables at a food court in Singapore on April 7, 2020. (Xinhua/Then Chih Wey)
