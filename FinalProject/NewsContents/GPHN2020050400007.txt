Title : Renfrew County confirmed coronavirus cases remain same May 2
Ariticle : 
As of noon on Saturday, May 2, the Renfrew County and District Health Unit is reporting 16 confirmed cases of COVID-19 (same as yesterday), including one death. Of these cases, 12 have been resolved, meaning there are only three confirmed active cases (the single death does not count as resolved).
See how this compares to elsewhere in the province with this interactive map.
A total of 1,667 tests have been conducted. Of these, 1,547 have come back negative, and 104 are still pending.
You can now access your COVID-19 lab test results online. For access, visit the Government of Ontario's online portal.
You do not require a phone assessment by RCDHU to access COVID-19 care.
Please complete this self-assessment tool to help you determine what to do.
Special considerations include: All persons over 70 years of age and individuals who are immunocompromised are advised to self-isolate.
All international travellers must immediately self-isolate for 14 days. If you are returning from Italy, Iran, or Hubei province (China), contact RCDHU at 613-735-8654 within 24 hours of arriving in Canada and provide your contact information. Other COVID-19 related information on workplaces and health care providers can be found at the respective links.
Please be aware outdoor recreation amenities are now closed, and the OPP are enforcing the laws that came of the province's declaration of emergency. There is a fine of at least $750 for failure to comply with an order made during a declared state of emergency, such as individuals gathering in groups of five or more and non-essential businesses remaining open.
If you believe a business is slowing efforts to stop the spread of COVID-19, call the OPP at 1-888-310-1122.
If you live in Renfrew County and District, have a health concern (including a concern related to COVID-19) and need to speak to a physician, you should first call your family physician's office.
If you do not have a family physician or cannot access your family physician, call the Renfrew County Virtual Triage and Assessment Centre at 1-844-727-6404. A medical receptionist will connect you with care and support from a nurse practitioner, family physician and/or community paramedic. This new service is covered by OHIP with a valid health card. Learn more at www.rcvtac.ca.
