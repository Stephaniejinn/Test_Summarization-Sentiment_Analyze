Title : Xinhua Photos of the Day (April 29)
Ariticle : 
Beijing, April 29 (Xinhua) -- The world in the past 24hrs. A selection of the best daily press photos from Xinhua.
Photo taken on April 25, 2020 from the Kya Wu Lha Pass shows the Mount Qomolangma at sunrise in southwest China's Tibet Autonomous Region. (Xinhua/Purbu Zhaxi)
Li Yicheng learns about the growth of cattle in Yaogao Village's high mountain natural pasture in Rongshui Miao Autonomous County, south China's Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, April 26, 2020.
Li Yicheng, now 39, voluntarily applied to come to Yaogao Village as a poverty alleviation team member in 2017. With the joint efforts of Li Yicheng and his colleagues, the village is lifted out of poverty in 2019. After this, Li plans to integrate Miao ethnic culture and local natural resources to promote cultural tourism industry. (Xinhua/Huang Xiaobang)
Pregnant wife Xu Yan wipes sweat for her husband Zhou Xiaolong in Baihua Town, Yibin City of southwest China's Sichuan Province, April 27, 2020.
The couple, who used to work in different cities, now live in the countryside and runs a live account with more than 3 million fans. The beautiful rural life they show in the video is popular among viewers. 
At the end of June, their baby is about to be born. Xu Yan plans to record the growth of the baby by video. The couple is optimistic about their future. (Xinhua/Li Mengxin)
Staff members hang small coral fragments at a coral nursery of the Wuzhizhou Island scenic area in Sanya, south China's Hainan Province, April 28, 2020. (Xinhua/Yang Guanyu)
Surfers walk at Bondi Beach in Sydney, Australia, on April 28, 2020. 
Hundreds of Aussies flocked to the water on Tuesday as the iconic Bondi Beach reopened for surfers and swimmers after being closed due to COVID-19. (Xinhua/Bai Xuefei)
The Fearless Girl statue with a face mask is seen in front of the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) during the COVID-19 pandemic in New York, the United States, on April 27, 2020.
The number of COVID-19 cases in the United States topped 1 million Tuesday afternoon, reaching 1,002,498 as of 1:55 p.m. (1755 GMT), according to the Center for Systems Science and Engineering at Johns Hopkins University. (Photo by Michael Nagle/Xinhua)
A formation of the Thunderbirds and Blue Angels fly over New York City, the United States, on April 28, 2020.
The U.S. Air Force Thunderbirds and the U.S. Navy Blue Angels honored frontline COVID-19 responders and essential workers with formation flights over New York City, Newark, Trenton and Philadelphia on Tuesday. (Xinhua/Wang Ying)
A COVID-19 sample collection kiosk is seen on a road during a government-imposed nationwide lockdown as a preventive measure against COVID-19 in Agartala, the capital city of India's northeastern state of Tripura, April 28, 2020. (Str/Xinhua)
Burundian President Pierre Nkurunziza (R, front) and Evariste Ndayishimiye (L, front), presidential candidate and secretary general of the ruling National Council for the Defense of Democracy-Forces for the Defense of Democracy (CNDDD-FDD) of Burundi, attend a campaign rally in Gitega, central Burundi, on April 27, 2020.
Burundi's political parties and groups and independent candidates on Monday deployed nationwide for a three-week campaign ahead of the upcoming general elections.
The African nation is set to elect new president, members of National Assembly and district councillors on May 20. (Photo by Evrard Ngendakumana/Xinhua)
Soldiers are seen during a Turkish-Russian land patrol on M-4 highway in northwestern Idlib province of Syria, on April 28, 2020.
Turkish and Russian troops on Tuesday held their sixth joint patrol along key M-4 highway in northwestern Idlib province of Syria, Turkish Defense Ministry said. (Xinhua)
People work at the construction site of Joy City building complex in Wuhan, central China's Hubei Province, April 28, 2020.
Over 800 workers from China Construction Third Engineering Bureau Co., Ltd. are busy with the construction work of the building complex. Most of them participated in the construction of the Huoshenshan and Leishenshan hospitals in Wuhan and have undergone a 14-day quarantine before they resumed to work. (Xinhua/Xiao Yijiu)
Photo taken on April 27, 2020 shows the Notre-Dame Cathedral under restoration in Paris, capital of France.
The renovation of Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris resumed on Monday after a suspension since March 17 due to the COVID-19 epidemic, the public agency overseeing the project announced. (Photo by Aurelien Morissard/Xinhua)
A cultural relics expert restores a mural at the No. 48 grotto of Xumishan Grottoes in Guyuan, northwest China's Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region, April 27, 2020.
Cultural relics experts from across China have begun a restoration project on murals in the Xumishan Grottoes dating back over 1,500 years in northwest China's Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region. 
The restoration project started in early April and is the first such project to save and restore Xumishan Grottoes murals since the Qing Dynasty (1644-1911), according to the cultural relics management bureau of Xumishan Grottoes. 
The Xumishan Grottoes, which were initially built in the late period of the Northern Wei Dynasty (386-534), have 162 caves and more than 1,000 statues and were listed as a key state-level cultural site in 1982. (Xinhua/Feng Kaihua)
Photo taken on April 28, 2020 shows a construction site of a highway tunnel in Shaoguan, south China's Guangdong Province. (Xinhua/Liu Dawei)
Photo taken on April 28, 2020 shows a view from a pagoda in Shibaozhai, a scenic spot in Zhongxian County, southwest China's Chongqing. (Xinhua/Tang Yi)
An egret flies over a forest park at sunset in Nanchang, east China's Jiangxi Province, April 27, 2020. (Xinhua/Wan Xiang)
