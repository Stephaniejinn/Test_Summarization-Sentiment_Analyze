Title : Massive door-to-door surveillance ongoing to identify SARI & ILI cases across Bengal : Mamata
Ariticle : 
Kolkata, May 5 -- West Bengal Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee on Tuesday said massive door-to-door surveillance has been ongoing since the past one month to identify Severe Acute Respiratory Illness ( SARI ) and Influenza-like Illness (ILI) cases across West Bengal.
"Massive door-to-door surveillance has been ongoing since the past one month to identify Severe Acute Respiratory Illness ( SARI ) & Influenza-like Illness (ILI) cases across West Bengal," Ms Banerjee posted on her social networking page.
"This Herculean effort has been undertaken by 60,000 specially trained ASHA & health workers non-stop for the past 4 weeks. The surveillance gives us early warning signals and is an important proactive step in fighting COVID19," she said.
The chief minister said, "During the period of 7th April to 3rd May, 5.57 Cr+ total household visits have been conducted. 872 cases of persons with SARI & 91,515 cases of persons with ILI have been identified and given necessary health advice. 375 people have also been admitted to different health facilities. 62 of them have tested positive for COVID-19 with the affected being treated at our Hospitals."
"The surveillance efforts are ongoing and we will not stop till we together defeat Corona in Bengal," she added.
With seven more deaths, the Covid -19 toll in West Bengal rose to 68 on Tuesday, while the fresh cases stood at 85.
Briefing newsmen at Nabanna, Chief Secretary Rajiva Sinha said 940 people were under treatment, while 46 people have already been released.
While 264 people have been cured after treatment, total cases stood at 1344, added Mr Sinha.
Meanwhile, a two-member central team arrived here today to study the overall situation in the wake of Covid-19 and implementation of the protocol in the fight against the deadly pandemic across this metropolis, which is one of the red zone of West Bengal.
The arrival of the All India Institute of Hygiene and Public Health senior medic Aprajita Dasgupta and public health expert Leena Banerjee happened a day after the two groups of from the Inter Ministerial Central Team ( IMCT) flew back to Delhi after two weeks of visit to south Bengal and north Bengal districts.
